I'm Sami Ben Hamida — a Finance Transformation & AI Integration specialist with over 15 years of experience in multinational pharmaceutical and services environments spanning Tunisia, Libya, Morocco, Jordan, UAE and beyond, now based in the UK.

GSF OpEx Monitoring Tool

Sanofi · MCO Region · Tunisia — 2017–2019
15 GSF Functions
3 Countries
€167M Scope
16 Controllers

As Head of Controlling for Sanofi's MCO region, one of my early priorities was to answer a question the CFO asked every month: "Where exactly are we losing ground against forecast — and who owns it?"

The existing reporting process relied on manual HFM extracts reconciled in individual Excel files by each function. There was no consolidated view, no accountability by function head, and no early warning system. By the time variances were visible at ExCo level, it was too late to act.

I designed and built the GSF Monitoring Tool — a multi-entity OpEx tracking system covering all 15 Global Support Functions across Tunisia, Libya and Morocco. Each function controller received access to their own module only, with a live HFM feed populating Budget (B19), Forecast (F1) and Actuals (A19) at the cost-nature level — 50+ cost codes tracked per function, per month.

The tool was the operational backbone of our Smart Spending initiative launched in Q1 2019, when the Executive Committee called for flat OpEx across 2019–2021. Without this monitoring layer, the savings action plan would have had no teeth.

How & Why I Built It
01
Problem diagnosis Variance reporting arrived 15+ days after month-end. No function-level ownership meant overruns were discovered too late. CFO escalations were reactive, not preventive.
02
Architecture decision TM1/EPM rollout was 18 months away. I needed a solution in weeks. I designed a VBA-governed Excel workbook with HFM CUBE data feeds — giving EPM-like consolidation within the existing tech stack.
03
Governance layer Built a role-based login system: each of the 16 function controllers could only access their own sheet. Admin view for CFO/Head of Controlling. This enforced accountability and data ownership.
04
Full chart of accounts Mapped all 50+ Sanofi cost nature codes (AA10 through ZD99) per function — from salaries and vehicle expenses to ITS depreciation and interco reinvoicing. Nothing lumped or hidden.
05
Smart Spending backbone The tool became the data source for the Q1 2019 ExCo call to action: -30% T&E, -50% training, -50% consulting, flat OpEx 2019–2021. Every savings target was tracked live against this tool.
06
Path to EPM This tool exposed exactly why a proper EPM was needed: no drill-through, fragile VBA, plaintext passwords. I used its limitations to build the business case for IBM Cognos TM1 — which I then led as client-side MOA.

Smart Spending — ExCo Call to Action

Sanofi · MCO Region · Tunisia & Morocco — Q1 2019
Flat OpEx 2019–2021
7 Spend Categories
2 Countries
ExCo Mandate

In early 2019, the Sanofi Executive Committee issued a direct call to action: achieve flat total OpEx across 2019, 2020 and 2021 — absorbing personal cost inflation of +6% (Tunisia) and +2.5% (Morocco) through savings in all other spend categories.

I led the Controlling team's response: a full spend mapping exercise across all commercial and support functions in Tunisia and Morocco, covering both OpEx and non-OpEx. Seven priority categories were identified — T&E, Training, Consulting, FM, Car Fleet, Data/Market Research, and Agency costs.

For each category, I built a vendor-level action plan: renegotiating contracts (car fleet leases, Orange telecom tenders, FM suppliers), challenging purchasing behaviours (T&E -30%, Training -50%), and restructuring service models (outsourcing cleaning, catering, maintenance). The 2018 spend mapping became the baseline; the GSF monitoring tool became the live tracking layer.

The initiative required me to work simultaneously as Finance Business Partner, project manager, and change agent — presenting to the GM, challenging function heads on their spend, and keeping Corporate France informed through structured governance reporting.

Approach & Methodology
01
Full spend mapping Mapped 100% of 2018 OpEx and Non-OpEx by BU (CEM, CHC, Vaccines, IA, GSF) and by category. Tunisia total: ~11.7M. Morocco total: ~29.4M.
02
Category action plans Developed vendor-specific plans for 7 categories. Each plan had a % target, a named vendor, a contract lever, and an owner. No category was left as "watch and see."
03
3-year financial model Built the flat OpEx model for Tunisia and Morocco: personal cost escalation offset against spend reductions, resulting in net flat total OpEx by 2021 — presented to CFO and GM.
04
Monthly tracking Integrated savings tracking into the GSF monitoring tool — every category had a live variance vs. target, reviewed in monthly Business Reviews with Corporate France.

IBM Cognos TM1 — From Excel to EPM

Sanofi · Libya Operations · Tunisia — 2013–2016
120 Users
€20M Market
3 Months → 3 Weeks
MOA Role

After establishing the full finance function for Sanofi's Libya operations from scratch — including building the management accounting framework, financial close, AP/AR and regulatory reporting with no prior infrastructure — the next challenge was transforming how the region planned and forecasted.

The annual budget cycle took 3 months: manual Excel templates sent to 50+ cost centre managers, consolidated by hand, reconciled against SAP, then re-submitted. Every assumption change triggered a new round. The process consumed the entire controlling team for a quarter of the year.

I was appointed client-side project lead (MOA) for the IBM Cognos TM1 implementation — a 120-user integrated planning platform spanning Finance, Supply Chain and Operations. Business & Decision was the integrator. I owned the functional design, business requirements, data model validation, and UAT — ensuring TM1 was built to reflect how we actually worked, not just how the system vendor assumed we would.

The outcome: budget cycle reduced from 3 months to 3 weeks. Driver-based planning replaced bottom-up manual entry. Automated workflows replaced email chains. The relationship built with Business & Decision on this project led directly to their recruiting me in 2020.

My Role as MOA (Client-Side Lead)
01
Business requirements ownership Translated finance team needs into functional specifications. Challenged the integrator where standard templates didn't fit Sanofi's planning logic.
02
SAP data model alignment Designed the TM1 data model to mirror SAP FI/CO architecture — eliminating reconciliation gaps between planning and actuals that had previously cost 2+ days per month.
03
Driver-based planning design Replaced line-by-line manual entry with driver logic: headcount × salary rate, volume × unit cost, etc. This meant a reforecast took hours, not weeks.
04
UAT & change management Led full UAT across Finance, Supply Chain and Operations. Ran adoption workshops. Became the internal escalation point post go-live.
