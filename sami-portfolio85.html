GSF OpEx Monitoring Tool

Sanofi · MCO Region · Tunisia — 2017–2019
15 GSF Functions
3 Countries
€167M Scope
16 Controllers

As Head of Controlling for the MCO region, one of my early priorities was to answer a question the CFO asked every month: "Where exactly are we losing ground against forecast — and who owns it?"

The existing process relied on manual HFM extracts in individual Excel files. No consolidated view, no accountability by function head, no early warning. By the time variances were visible at ExCo level, it was too late to act.

I designed and built the GSF Monitoring Tool — a multi-entity OpEx tracking system covering all 15 Global Support Functions across Tunisia, Libya and Morocco, with live HFM data feeds populating Budget, Forecast and Actuals at cost-nature level.

The tool became the backbone of the Smart Spending initiative launched in Q1 2019 — flat OpEx 2019–2021. Without this monitoring layer, the savings plan would have had no teeth.

How & Why I Built It
01
Problem diagnosisVariance reporting arrived 15+ days after month-end. No function-level ownership meant overruns were discovered too late.
02
Architecture decisionTM1 rollout was 18 months away. I needed a solution in weeks — VBA-governed Excel with HFM CUBE feeds gave EPM-like consolidation within the existing stack.
03
Governance layerRole-based login: each of 16 controllers could only access their own sheet. Admin view for CFO. Enforced accountability and data ownership.
04
Path to EPMThis tool exposed exactly why TM1 was needed. I used its limitations to build the business case — and then led TM1 as client-side MOA.

🎯 Strategic Finance📉 Cost Transformation

Smart Spending — ExCo Call to Action

Sanofi · MCO Region · Tunisia & Morocco — Q1 2019
Flat OpEx 2019–2021
7 Categories
ExCo Mandate

In early 2019, the ExCo issued a direct call to action: achieve flat total OpEx across 2019, 2020 and 2021 — absorbing personal cost inflation of +6% (Tunisia) and +2.5% (Morocco) through savings in all other categories.

I led a full spend mapping exercise across all commercial and support functions in Tunisia and Morocco, covering OpEx and non-OpEx. Seven priority categories identified — T&E, Training, Consulting, FM, Car Fleet, Data/Market Research, and Agency costs.

For each category I built a vendor-level action plan: renegotiating contracts, challenging purchasing behaviours, restructuring service models. The GSF monitoring tool became the live tracking layer.

Approach
01
Full spend mappingMapped 100% of 2018 OpEx and Non-OpEx by BU across Tunisia (~11.7M) and Morocco (~29.4M).
02
Category action plansVendor-specific plans for 7 categories — each with a % target, named vendor, contract lever, and owner.
03
3-year financial modelBuilt the flat OpEx model for Tunisia and Morocco — presented to CFO and GM. Approved and tracked monthly.

I was appointed client-side project lead (MOA) for the IBM Cognos TM1 implementation — a 120-user integrated planning platform across Finance, Supply Chain and Operations. I owned functional design, business requirements, data model validation and UAT.
